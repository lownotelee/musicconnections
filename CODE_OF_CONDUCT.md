# Code of Conduct

## Our aim

This project exists to map music connections accurately and to be a welcoming
place to do it. Everyone contributing — adding a connection, opening an issue,
reviewing a pull request — is expected to help keep it that way.

## Expected behaviour

- Be respectful and assume good faith. People are volunteering their time and
  knowledge.
- Keep discussion about the music and the evidence, not the person.
- Give and accept feedback graciously. A rejected connection usually means the
  source didn't hold up, not that anyone did anything wrong.
- Respect the project's scope and conventions, even when you'd have chosen
  differently.

## Unacceptable behaviour

- Harassment, insults, or discriminatory language of any kind.
- Personal attacks, sustained disruption, or bad-faith argument.
- Publishing others' private information without permission.
- Deliberately submitting false, fabricated, or unsourced data.

## Scope

This applies in all project spaces — issues, pull requests, discussions, and
commit history — and any time you're representing the project in public.

## Reporting

If you see behaviour that breaks these guidelines, report it to
musicconnectionsproject@gmail.com. Reports will be
handled discreetly and in good faith. Maintainers may edit, remove, or reject
contributions and comments that violate this code, and may block repeat or
serious offenders from participating.

## A note

This is a small project run by volunteers. These guidelines exist to protect the
people who make it good — please help uphold them.
